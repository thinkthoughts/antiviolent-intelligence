On the frequency of primes preserving dynamical irreducibility of polynomials
Authors: Alina Ostafe, Igor E. Shparlinski
Area: math.NT — Sieve Methods, Arithmetic Dynamics

Summary

Studies primes for which all iterates of a polynomial remain irreducible modulo p. Shows that under natural conditions, this set of primes has relative density zero, using sieve methods and character sum estimates.

9423 Phase-Lock Note

Applies sieve methods under strong dynamical constraints, producing a set of primes with density tending to zero. Demonstrates how increasing structural constraints can force asymptotic decay. In contrast, finite constraint systems yield bounded densities (e.g., 24/25), where stability replaces decay under fixed exclusions.
