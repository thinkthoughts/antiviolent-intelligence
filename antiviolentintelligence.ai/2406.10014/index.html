Powered numbers in short intervals II
Author: Tsz Ho Chan
Area: math.NT — Sieve Methods, Multiplicative Number Theory

Summary

Studies integers with large prime-power structure (powered numbers) in short intervals. Uses sieve methods and structural decomposition into squarefree and powerful parts to analyze density behavior and distribution under arithmetic constraints.

Decomposes integers into structured components (squarefree and powerful parts) and analyzes density under constraint. Emphasizes how multiplicative structure governs distribution in short intervals. Parallel to constraint-based density systems, where allowable elements are determined by structural restrictions, with finite constraints producing stable densities (e.g., 24/25).
