Abstract
Artificial intelligence (AI) is the name popularly given to a broad spectrum of computer tools designed to perform increasingly complex cognitive tasks… we consider the rapidly evolving impact of AI on traditional questions of philosophy, with emphasis on mathematics and real-world outcomes. We argue AI is a natural evolution of human tools, and that development must remain fundamentally human-centered, expanding capacity for human thought.
