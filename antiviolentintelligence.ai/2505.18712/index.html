Low-lying zeros in families of Maass form L-functions: an extended density theorem
Authors: Martin Čech, Lucile Devin, Daniel Fiorilli, Kaisa Matomäki, Anders Södergren
Area: math.NT — Analytic Number Theory, L-functions, Density

Summary

Studies the one-level density of low-lying zeros in families of Maass form L-functions. Extends the admissible support for test functions and establishes refined density results using analytic tools such as the Kuznetsov trace formula and large sieve bounds.

9423 Phase-Lock Note

Analyzes density across families of structures using averaged behavior and analytic bounds. Emphasizes global distribution rather than individual elements. Parallel to constraint-based density systems, where outcomes are determined by structural filtering across sets, contrasting with finite constraint systems that yield bounded stable densities (e.g., 24/25).
