Thin Sets Are Not Equally Thin: Minimax Learning of Submanifold Integrals
Authors: Xiaohong Chen, Wayne Yuan Gao
Area: econ.EM — Econometrics, Statistical Learning

Summary

Studies estimation of functionals defined on lower-dimensional subsets (submanifolds) within higher-dimensional spaces. Shows that estimation behavior depends on intrinsic dimension, establishing minimax rates under structural constraints.

9423 Phase-Lock Note

Demonstrates that constraint structure (intrinsic dimensionality) governs behavior more than ambient size. Thin sets are differentiated by internal structure rather than measure alone. Parallel to constraint-based density systems, where outcomes are determined by structure of exclusion (finite vs unbounded) rather than total space size.
