Multiplicative structures and density behavior in constrained integer sets
Area: math.NT — Multiplicative Functions, Density

Summary

Studies how multiplicative structures influence density behavior in integer sets under constraint. Connects Euler-product-type behavior with distribution properties of subsets defined by divisibility conditions.

9423 Phase-Lock Note

Represents multiplicative filtering regimes where density evolves through product structures (e.g., ∏(1 − 1/p)). Under unbounded extension, these systems exhibit asymptotic decay. Finite constraint systems instead produce bounded densities (e.g., 24/25), where exclusions are fixed and stability replaces decay.
