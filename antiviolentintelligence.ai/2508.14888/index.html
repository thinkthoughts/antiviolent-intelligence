Large sieves for GLₙ and applications
Authors: Alexandru Pascadi, Jesse Thorner
Area: math.NT — Analytic Number Theory, Sieve Methods

Summary

Develops large sieve inequalities for families of automorphic representations on GLₙ. Establishes bounds for L-functions and zero-density estimates, extending classical sieve methods into higher-dimensional analytic number theory.

9423 Phase-Lock Note

Treats sieve methods as a framework for controlling families of structures through bounded interactions. Density behavior emerges from global constraints across sets rather than individual elements. Parallel to constraint-based density systems, where stability arises from bounded structure, contrasting with unbounded multiplicative filtering that leads to asymptotic decay.
