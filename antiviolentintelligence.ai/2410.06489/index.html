High proton conductivity through angstrom-porous titania
Authors: Y. Ji et al.
Area: cond-mat.mtrl-sci — Materials Science, Transport

Summary

Studies proton transport through vacancy-rich titania monolayers. Demonstrates that atomic-scale defects form angstrom-sized pores that allow selective proton transport while blocking other particles, achieving high conductivity and strong selectivity.

9423 Phase-Lock Note

Implements a physical sieve: atomic-scale structure determines which particles are transmitted or blocked. Selectivity arises from constraint geometry rather than global properties. Parallel to constraint-based density systems, where allowed elements are determined by structural exclusion, producing stable, predictable distributions under fixed constraints.
