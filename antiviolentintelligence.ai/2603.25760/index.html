Topology as a Language for Emergent Organization in Complex Systems
Author: Mark M. Bailey
Area: physics.soc-ph — Complex Systems, Topological Data Analysis

Summary

Reviews how topological methods such as persistent homology and higher-order representations capture multiscale organization in complex systems. Argues that topology provides a mathematical language for describing structure that is distributed, relational, and robust across scale.

9423 Phase-Lock Note

Emphasizes that structure is defined by relationships that persist across scale rather than individual components. Topological methods identify stable organizational features under changing resolution. Parallel to constraint-based density systems, where allowable structure under constraint determines stable distributions (e.g., 24/25), and filtering reveals what persists within the system.
